All about detectives!

The detective genre features narratives centered on crime-solving and mystery. It showcases astute investigators unraveling complex cases through keen observation and deductive reasoning. Popularized by characters like Sherlock Holmes and Hercule Poirot, the genre combines suspense, intrigue, and intellectual challenge, captivating audiences with tales of justice and clever resolution.
Detectives are skilled investigators specializing in solving crimes and mysteries. They gather and analyze evidence, interview witnesses, and follow leads to uncover the truth. Often working for police departments or as private investigators, detectives use critical thinking, attention to detail, and knowledge of criminal behavior to solve cases. Famous fictional detectives like Sherlock Holmes and Hercule Poirot have popularized their profession, showcasing their deductive prowess. Real-life detectives play a crucial role in maintaining law and order, bringing criminals to justice, and providing closure to victims and their families. Their work often involves collaboration with other law enforcement agencies and experts.

Sherlock Holmes

Sherlock Holmes, created by Sir Arthur Conan Doyle, is a legendary detective known for his brilliant deductive skills and keen observation. Residing at 221B Baker Street, he solves intricate cases with his companion Dr. Watson. Holmes' iconic intellect and methodical approach have made him a timeless figure in detective fiction.

The first detective story was written in 1841 by Edgar Allan - "Murder in the Rue Morgue".
